What is Global Warming?

Global warming refers to the gradual increase in the Earth's average temperature due to the rise in greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere and create a "greenhouse effect" that warms the planet.

Over the past century, human activities like burning fossil fuels, industrial development, and deforestation have significantly increased the concentration of these gases in the air. As a result, the Earth's climate system is changing at an alarming rate.

Global warming is not just about temperature rise. It affects weather patterns, ecosystems, oceans, and even human health. It is considered one of the biggest environmental challenges of the 21st century.
